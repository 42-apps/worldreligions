World Religions Explorer

A living map of the world's faiths. Each country is coloured by its majority religion; hover for the full proportional mix. Drag the slider — or hit play — to watch the religious landscape change from antiquity to today, with projections to 2100.

Please read: figures are approximate and illustrative. Modern data (1945–2020) draws on Pew Research and the World Religion Project; deep-history figures are best-effort scholarly estimates. The map uses modern borders at all eras as a visualization device — ancient peoples and empires, not today's nations, occupied that land. Composition counts resident populations (including expatriates), as Pew does.

Sources: Pew Research Center · World Religion Project (Maoz & Henderson, ARDA) · Our World in Data · historical scholarship. Borders: Natural Earth. Globe: globe.gl.

🗺️ The flat map

Every country is filled proportionally by faith — so you see each nation's whole religious mix at a glance, not just its majority.

📊
Proportional fill. Each country fills from the bottom up by share. A nation that's 70% Christian & 30% Muslim shows the bottom 70% purple, the top 30% green — the taller a colour, the bigger that faith.
🔍
Zoom & pan. Scroll to zoom in (small countries become readable) and drag to move around. Recentre via ☰ → Reset view.
👆
Hover any country for its exact percentages; click for the full breakdown and a trend chart over time.
🎨
Highlight a faith — click one in the left panel to shade the whole map by that religion's share. Globe view is in the ☰ menu.

This is a flat (equirectangular) projection, so areas near the poles look stretched (Greenland, Antarctica), and a couple of countries that cross the date line (Russia, Fiji) can smear sideways.
